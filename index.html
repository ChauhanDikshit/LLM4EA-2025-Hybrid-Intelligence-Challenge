Overview & Theme

The LLM4EA 2025 Challenge brings together the power of Large Language Models (LLMs) and Evolutionary Algorithms (EAs) to develop next-generation optimization tools. This hybrid approach seeks to transform algorithm design, parameter adaptation, and real-world problem solving through the lens of intelligent language models.

Objectives

  • Explore synergies between LLMs and EAs for hybrid optimization.
  • Encourage the design of intelligent metaheuristics using LLMs.
  • Benchmark and promote effective strategies for language-informed evolution.
  • Foster global collaboration in hybrid AI development.

Scope & Tracks

  • Track 1: Development of LLM-Guided Metaheuristic
  • Track 2: Apply LLM-Guided Metaheuristics on the CEC 2017 Bound Constrained Problems

Rules & Guidelines

  • Each team may submit one entry per track.
  • Submissions must include code, a short report (max 4 pages), and performance logs.
  • LLMs must play an active role in the solution pipeline (e.g., operator selection, prompt generation, evaluation, etc.).
  • Plagiarism or rule violations may lead to disqualification.

Evaluation Criteria

  • Originality and innovation in hybrid approach
  • Optimization performance on benchmark problems
  • Clarity and quality of implementation
  • Scalability and generalization of the method
  • Explainability of LLM usage in the pipeline

Submission Requirements

  • Source code (well-commented and reproducible)
  • Short report (PDF, 4 pages max)
  • README file with setup and instructions
  • Benchmark results for designated problems
